**Show name:** Between the Frames  
**Tagline:** *Real conversations about visual storytelling, business, and the space between*  
**Host:** Xavier Clarke, XC Photography  
**Format:** Weekly interview show + behind-the-scenes vlog content  
**Primary platform:** YouTube  
**Distribution:** Podcast (audio strip), LinkedIn Live, Instagram clips, website

---

## WHY THIS WORKS FOR XC PHOTOGRAPHY

Adorama's "Coffee with Creators" proved a model: a working photographer hosting honest, casual conversations with other photographers and visual creators builds enormous authority, community, and discoverability — without selling anything.

The show isn't advertising. The show *is* the brand.

For Xavier Clarke, "Between the Frames" does several things simultaneously:

1. **Positions Xavier as an industry voice**, not just a service provider. Clients hire the people whose thinking they respect.
2. **Creates 52 pieces of long-form content per year** — each generating a YouTube video, podcast episode, blog summary, LinkedIn post, Instagram clips, and a newsletter issue.
3. **Builds genuine relationships with guests** — who often become referrers, collaborators, or clients.
4. **Targets London and Brighton creative communities** — featuring local and national guests creates geographic relevance and local SEO signal.
5. **Generates YouTube SEO** — "brand photography," "documentary photographer," "photographer interview" searches bring new audiences.
6. **Gives clients a window into Xavier's thinking** — the best salespeople are the episodes themselves.

**"Between the Frames" adaptation:**

### Episode Run-of-Show (35-50 minutes)

**[00:00–02:00] Cold open**  
30-second clip of the best moment from today's episode — no intro, no logo, straight into the most interesting/surprising thing a guest says. This is the YouTube hook. Cut to title card.

**[02:00–05:00] Welcome + guest introduction**  
Xavier introduces himself briefly (not the channel — people can scroll down for that). Introduces the guest: who they are, what they do, why they're worth 45 minutes of a viewer's Thursday.

Guest does NOT read a bio. Xavier gives it in his own words, from genuine knowledge of the guest's work.

**[05:00–15:00] The origin story**  
How did they get here? Not a CV — the actual story. The turn that mattered. The failure that changed things. The unexpected moment.

*Adorama's formula: the guest's creative journey, told honestly. The bits that didn't work matter more than the bits that did.*

**[15:00–30:00] The work — deep dive**  
One project, one area of expertise, one thing they've built or discovered that's genuinely interesting. Xavier has done the research. This is not "tell me about your work" — it's "I noticed you changed how you approach [X] — talk me through that decision."

For brand/business guests: how visual storytelling has impacted their business  
For photographer guests: their specific approach, what distinguishes their work  
For creative directors: what they look for, what bad photography costs, what good photography enables

**[30:00–40:00] The honest bit**  
What's actually hard about this. What they got wrong. What they'd do differently. What the industry gets wrong that they've stopped pretending is fine.

*This is the section that drives shares. Adorama guests' most-clipped moments are always the moments of honest discomfort.*

**[40:00–47:00] Audience Q&A**  
Live questions from LinkedIn/YouTube. Xavier reads them, edits for clarity, credits the asker.

**[47:00–50:00] Where to find them + close**  
Guest: where to follow, what's coming next.  
Xavier: one sentence about what listeners can do if today resonated (book a call, follow the show, share with someone who needs it).  
No hard sell. No promotional monologue.

## GUEST STRATEGY

### The Three Guest Tiers

**Tier 1 — Brand owners and business leaders (primary audience clients)**  
London and Brighton business owners, founders, marketing directors who have strong opinions about visual branding. These guests speak directly to XC Photography's ideal client.

*Why this works:* Other business owners watch their peers. A Brighton founder or a London creative director talking about how brand photography transformed their business is the most authentic possible testimonial — and it's 45 minutes long.

*Outreach approach:* Find them on LinkedIn. Genuine engagement with their content for 2-3 weeks before asking. The ask is a genuine compliment about their perspective + a specific reason their story would resonate with the audience.

**Tier 2 — Other photographers and visual creators**  
Working photographers — brand, documentary, event, editorial — whose approach and perspective are distinctive. Not competitors (there are no competitors if you're good enough at your niche) — collaborators in the broader conversation about visual storytelling.

*Why this works:* Photographer guests attract photographer audiences, which includes future referrers. Also: the business-owner audience finds "how a photographer thinks" genuinely fascinating.

**Tier 3 — Industry adjacent guests**  
Brand strategists, marketing professionals, art directors, web designers, social media managers — people who commission photography, work alongside it, or think about visual branding from a different angle.

*Why this works:* These guests speak to the business case that Xavier's clients need to make internally to justify the investment.

## PRODUCTION SETUP

### Minimum Viable Setup (Launch Phase)

The goal at launch is **consistency over quality**. Adorama's Coffee with Creators started simply. The production values improved with the audience, not before it.

**Equipment:**
- Camera: Any mirrorless or DSLR Xavier already owns (this is not a gear problem)
- Audio: Rode Wireless GO II or similar (two-person lapel mics) — audio is the #1 quality signal
- Lighting: One good key light (Aputure 120d or equivalent) + natural window light fills
- Background: Xavier's workspace, or a deliberately chosen coffee shop / studio

**Software:**
- Recording: OBS Studio (free) or StreamYard (from £29/month) for live streaming
- Editing: DaVinci Resolve (free) — export 35-50 min full episode + 60-90 second clip for YouTube Shorts/Instagram Reels
- Thumbnail: Canva Pro (already likely in use)

**Location rotation:**
- Brighton sessions: Xavier's studio or North Laine coffee shop
- London sessions: Book a WeWork meeting room or coworking space day pass — the London location signals to London clients that Xavier is genuinely present there
- Online: Riverside.fm or Zencastr for remote guests (HD video + isolated audio tracks)

## BEHIND-THE-SCENES VLOG CONTENT

Alongside the main interview show, a lighter BTS vlog format runs on YouTube and Instagram. This is the "day in the life" content that Adorama's channel also produces between interview episodes.

### XC Photography Vlog Series Concepts

**"A Day On Location"**  
Follow a real shoot day — client arrival prep, the session itself (with permission), the debrief, the drive back. No polished narrative. Real work, real conversations, real light.

*Why it works:* Demystifies the process. Potential clients see exactly what working with Xavier looks like. Existing clients feel seen. Competitors become less scary because the audience understands the craft.

**"Scout + Shoot"**  
Xavier scouts a location in London or Brighton — explaining what he's looking for, how the light works at different times, what he'd use each corner for. Then shoots something at that location.

*London angle:* Regularly scouting London locations signals to London clients that Xavier is a genuine London-capable photographer, not someone who occasionally travels up.

**"The Brief"**  
A fictional-but-realistic client brief is described. Xavier works through it — how he'd plan it, what he'd ask, what he'd worry about, how he'd approach the shoot. Shot as a talking-head video with BTS illustrations.

**"Between Sessions"**  
What happens when Xavier isn't shooting — editing workflow, gear maintenance, client communication, invoicing, business admin. The honest reality of running a creative business. Resonates with other photographers; humanises the brand for clients.

**"Location: London"**  
Monthly: one episode dedicated entirely to shooting in London. Different borough each time. Demonstrates London presence, creates locally relevant content.

## LAUNCH PLAN

### Pre-launch (Weeks 1-4)
- Record first 3 episodes before launching (never launch with just 1)
- Set up YouTube channel, Spotify/Apple podcast
- Create channel art and consistent thumbnail template
- Write blog post announcing the show
- Tease the show on LinkedIn for 2 weeks before launch: "Something new is coming. Every Thursday. About brand photography, business, and the space between."

### Launch Week
- Publish Episodes 1, 2, and 3 simultaneously (gives new subscribers something to binge)
- LinkedIn announcement post with genuine personal story about why this show is happening
- Email any past clients to tell them (social proof + built-in first audience)
- Share guest's episode with them so they share it to their own network

### Post-launch cadence
- One new episode every Thursday (non-negotiable — Adorama's consistency is what built their audience)
- Weekly LinkedIn post drawing on the episode content
- Monthly LinkedIn Live (live version of a shorter episode, building to the main channel)
- Quarterly review: which episodes performed best? Why? Do more of that.

## THE BETWEEN THE FRAMES PODCAST

The audio-only version of the show, stripped from the YouTube recording. Published same day as the video.

**Why a podcast:**
- Reaches audiences who consume while driving, exercising, commuting (London commuters = huge audience)
- Podcast listeners are statistically more engaged and more likely to convert
- RSS feeds distribute automatically to Spotify, Apple, Google Podcasts, Amazon Music
- Jeff Brown has a significant podcast presence — this is a core channel for photographer authority building

**Setup:** Export audio track from video edit → upload to Buzzsprout or Podbean (from £7/month) → auto-distributes to all platforms.

**Show notes:** The blog summary post doubles as podcast show notes.
