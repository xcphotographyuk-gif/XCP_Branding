### WORKSHOP 1 — LONDON: "THE MONOPOLY RUN"
**Documentary Photography from the Back of a Routemaster**

**Concept**  
A curated day aboard a hired Routemaster bus navigating iconic Monopoly board locations across London — Mayfair, Marylebone, Whitechapel, Liverpool Street, King's Cross — photographing each neighbourhood's character, light, and human stories. Couples, friends, and solo participants welcome. Hop on and off at stops, shoot freely, regroup on the bus for on-the-spot reviews.

This is not a standard photo walk. It is a moving classroom with one of the UK's most distinctive backdrops — and one of the most photogenic vehicles ever built.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Duration | Full day (approx. 9am–6pm including bus travel) |
| Group size | Maximum 10 |
| Price | £595 per person / £995 per couple |
| Location | London — Monopoly board route (start: Liverpool Street or Marylebone area) |
| Includes | Routemaster hire, route map, shot list for each location, on-bus reviews, printed contact sheet from the day |
| Frequency | Quarterly (seasonal light: spring and autumn particularly strong) |

**What participants leave with:**
- 100+ images shot across multiple London neighbourhoods
- Ability to read a location and find a picture in under 60 seconds
- Understanding of available light across interior/exterior/transit environments
- A shared set of images from the bus and the streets — every participant gets a full contact sheet from the day

**Logistics notes:**
- Routemaster hire: heritage vehicle hire companies operate in London — typically £600–£900/half day, £1,200–£1,800/full day. Source via Heritage Routemaster Hire or similar.
- Route to confirm: 10–12 Monopoly board stops mapped as a circular route, roughly 2–3 hours of driving with stops
- Backup plan for rain: the Routemaster itself becomes the shoot — portraits inside, shooting from the open rear platform

**Marketing hook:**  
*"Ten stops. One bus. Every London you've never photographed."*

**Revenue:**  
10 participants × £595 = £5,950 gross. After Routemaster hire (~£1,500), venue/admin (~£200): net ~£4,250.

### WORKSHOP 2 — BRIGHTON: "SOUND & CELLULOID"
**Brighton Music & Movies Photography Walk**

**Concept**  
Brighton's streets are a film set that nobody locked. From the alley behind St James's Street where *Quadrophenia* was filmed, to the Duke of York's Picturehouse (the UK's oldest cinema), to graffiti depicting iconic musicians on Kensington Gardens and beyond — this walk follows the city's musical and cinematic history through a camera lens.

Ideally includes a short guided tour inside the Duke of York's (to be arranged in advance with management) and a mapped route through the city's key music and film locations.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Duration | Half-day walk (4 hours) + optional evening gig or screening |
| Group size | Maximum 8 |
| Price | £295 per person |
| Location | Brighton — meeting point: Duke of York's Picturehouse, Preston Circus |
| Includes | Route map with location notes, historical context cards, post-walk online gallery of all participants' images |
| Frequency | Monthly (spring–autumn); bi-monthly in winter |

**Route outline:**
1. Duke of York's Picturehouse — architecture, history, interior light (subject to access)
2. Quadrophenia alley — St James's Street area, narrow lanes, The Who mural area
3. Kemp Town — music history, seafront light, graffiti walls
4. North Laine — independent venues, street art, record shops
5. Concorde 2 exterior — Brighton's iconic live music venue (connection to Xavier's own portfolio)
6. Brighton Pier — end point for golden hour shooting if timing allows

**What participants leave with:**
- Understanding how to shoot on a walk (composition, quick reactions, available light)
- Portfolio-quality documentary images of an iconic city
- Printed postcard from the walk — one image per participant selected and printed same-day (if print lab is in budget)

**Marketing hook:**  
*"Every corner has a story. Bring a camera."*

**Revenue:**  
8 participants × £295 = £2,360 gross. Net after route/admin: ~£1,900.

### WORKSHOP 3 — BRIGHTON: "TRAINS, PLANES & AUTOMOBILES"
**Nature, City, Sea — Three Worlds in One Day**

**Concept**  
Three very different environments. Three very different photographic challenges. One day.

Morning at the **Bluebell Railway** (East Grinstead) — heritage steam trains, platform portraits, light through steam, movement and stillness. Afternoon at **Shoreham Airport** (Brighton City Airport) — one of the UK's oldest airports, art deco terminal, light aircraft, open skies. Late afternoon/early evening back in Brighton for a sea and city section — piers, marina, urban contrast.

The car element: exploring options including Brighton's classic car scene — the London–Brighton Veteran Car Run ends in Brighton each November and is a credible anchor for an autumn edition of this workshop.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Duration | Full day (approx. 8:30am–6:30pm with travel) |
| Group size | Maximum 8 |
| Price | £495 per person |
| Location | East Sussex circuit — Bluebell Railway / Shoreham Airport / Brighton |
| Transport | Participants make own way between locations OR minibus hire as premium option |
| Frequency | Quarterly (spring and autumn for Bluebell Railway; autumn for Car Run edition) |

**What participants leave with:**
- Proficiency shooting across three radically different environments in a single day
- Understanding of motion, depth of field, and light management across open/indoor/transit spaces
- 200+ images covering heritage, aviation, and coastal documentary

**Variations:**
- **Autumn edition:** Bluebell Railway + Brighton Veteran Car Run + coast (November only)
- **Summer edition:** Shoreham Airport + sea swim photography / seaside documentary + North Laine
- **Train only:** Half-day Bluebell Railway intensive — for participants who want to master motion and platform shooting specifically

**Marketing hook:**  
*"Three worlds. One day. No two images the same."*

**Revenue:**  
8 participants × £495 = £3,960 gross. After Bluebell Railway admission (~£200 group), transport (~£200), admin: net ~£3,200.

### WORKSHOP 4 — BRIGHTON: "MODS WEEKENDER"
**Street Photography, Scooter Culture & Studio Lighting**

**Concept**  
Brighton's Mod scene is not nostalgia — it is a living, breathing subculture. The Mods Weekender (typically held twice a year in Brighton) is one of the UK's largest gatherings of Vespa and Lambretta riders, parkas, and sharp suits. This workshop runs in parallel with the weekender.

Morning: street and documentary photography during the scooter parade and seafront gathering — learning to work a crowd, find portraits, and shoot in flat/overcast English light.
Afternoon: studio pop-up — Xavier sets up a portable two-light studio setup at a venue (pub function room or seafront gallery) and participants rotate through shooting their own styled portraits and being directed as subjects.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Duration | Full day (approx. 9am–6pm) |
| Group size | Maximum 8 |
| Price | £395 per person |
| Location | Brighton — seafront and pop-up studio (venue TBC — Mod-friendly pub or gallery) |
| Includes | Street shoot in the morning, studio session in the afternoon, lighting setup breakdown |
| Frequency | Twice yearly — aligned to Brighton Mods Weekender dates |

**What participants leave with:**
- Ability to move from documentary (uncontrolled) to studio (controlled) environments in the same day
- Practical studio lighting experience with two-light setup
- Documentary images from one of the UK's most characterful street events

**Marketing hook:**  
*"From the seafront to the studio. One day. Two very different kinds of light."*

**Revenue:**  
8 participants × £395 = £3,160 gross. After venue hire (~£200), admin: net ~£2,600.

Two format options:

**Half-day (4 hours) — Introduction:**

Above + Rembrandt/loop/split lighting patterns, multi-light setups, gels and creative colour, shooting for post-production, tethered shooting workflow.

| Element | Detail |

| Group size | Maximum 6 (studio space constraint — intentional) |

| Location | Brighton studio space (day hire) or Xavier's home studio setup |
| Frequency | Monthly (half-day); bi-monthly (full-day) |

**Marketing hook:**
