# XC Photography — From Start to Success
## The complete guide. In order. No padding.

**Target:** £30,000–£60,000 per month within 18 months.  
**Minimum rate:** £1,495 per session — never lower.  
**This guide takes you from the moment you open your laptop tomorrow to a thriving, fully operational photography business.**

---

## THE ONLY THING TO UNDERSTAND BEFORE YOU START

You are not selling photographs. You are selling what the photographs do.

Your clients don't want a folder of JPEGs. They want to stop feeling embarrassed when they send people to their website. They want their visual presence to match the quality of what they actually deliver. They want their ideal clients to arrive already trusting them. That is what you sell. Every piece of this business — the website, the outreach, the blog posts, the workshops — is built around that single idea.

Your positioning: premium, documentary, edge. The "seedy side of luxury." Not generic corporate, not sanitised and safe. Brands and individuals who live at the luxury end but aren't the polished, boring version of it. Music. Hospitality. Nightlife. Creative industries. Cannabis. Advocacy. Anything that needs to look world-class without looking like everyone else.

You already have the portfolio to prove it. Norman Cook. Amazon. Hotel du Vin. The BBC. The Ivy. The Royal Pavilion. i360. Eddie Izzard. Goldie. Norman Jay OBE. Dean Chalkley. The Beach Boys. Hugh Cornwell (The Stranglers). Steve Norman (Spandau Ballet). That is not a new photographer trying to find clients. That is an established photographer relaunching with clarity.

Now. In order.

This is the fastest path to your first paid booking. Not social media. Not SEO. People who already know you.

**The email (from `WARM_OUTREACH_TEMPLATES.md`):**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Quick update — I've been working on relaunch and I'm taking on new brand photography clients from [this month].
>
> If you or anyone you know needs professional imagery — headshots, team, brand content — I have availability. Sessions from £1,495 for half a day.

### WEEK 2–3 — Activate WooCommerce print sales (one afternoon)

All print and shop sales go through WooCommerce on xcphotography.co. This means one payment gateway (Stripe), one order system, and no commission to a third party. Set it up once and it runs indefinitely.

**Full setup guide:** `WOOCOMMERCE_INTEGRATION_GUIDE.md`  
**Product import:** `WOOCOMMERCE_PRODUCT_IMPORT_FAST_TRACK.md`  
**Ready-made product list:** `SHOP_PRODUCTS_CSV_WOOCOMMERCE.csv`

**The short version:**
1. Install WooCommerce plugin (WordPress → Plugins → Add New → WooCommerce)
2. Connect Stripe as payment gateway (WooCommerce → Settings → Payments → Stripe)
3. Import products from `SHOP_PRODUCTS_CSV_WOOCOMMERCE.csv` (WooCommerce → Products → Import)
4. Connect Prodigi for print fulfilment: prodigi.com → connect WooCommerce store → map products to Prodigi print SKUs
5. Add a "Shop" or "Prints" link to your site navigation
6. On the Portfolio page, add a "Buy a print" button under your best images, linking to the relevant WooCommerce product

**Prodigi handles printing, fulfilment, and UK delivery. You receive the margin between your sale price and Prodigi's cost. No studio, no packing, no postage.**

SmugMug will be used for portfolio display and client gallery delivery in Phase 2 — after the main WordPress/Elementor site is fully functional. It is not needed for the WooCommerce print shop to work.

---

### WEEK 4 — Install and configure the Lightroom plugin

Every image you deliver should be properly keyworded, titled, and ready for the platform it's going to. The Lightroom plugin automates this.

**Install:**
1. Lightroom Classic → File → Plug-in Manager → Add
2. Navigate to `XCPImageTagger.lrplugin` folder → Add Plug-in
3. Should show "XCP Platform Publisher — Installed and running"

**Configure brand settings first:**
1. Library → Plug-in Extras → Brand Voice Settings
2. Set: Photographer Credit (Xavier Clarke / XC Photography), Instagram handle, website, location (Brighton, United Kingdom), Brand Voice (Documentary & Authentic)
3. Save Settings

**Workflow going forward — for every shoot:**
1. Select prepared images in Library
2. Library → Plug-in Extras → Multi-Platform Preparation
3. Tick the platforms you're preparing for (website + Instagram minimum)
4. Click Prepare All
5. All metadata written: title, caption, alt text, keywords, IPTC fields
6. Platform content stored in Instructions field — copy/paste to each destination

Full usage guide: `XCPImageTagger.lrplugin/README.md`

### LAUNCH YOUR FIRST WORKSHOP

Workshops do four things at once: revenue, authority, community, and content. One full workshop generates material for weeks of social posts and multiple client relationships.

Full strategy: `WORKSHOPS_EVENTS_STRATEGY.md`

**Start with the half-day format. It's the lowest-risk entry:**

| Detail | |
|---|---|
| Format | Half-Day Intensive (4 hours) |
| Group size | Maximum 8 |
| Price | Brighton £295 / London £395 per person |
| Content | Brand photography principles, natural light, directing your subjects, editing basics |
| What they leave with | 5+ images shot during the workshop, a personal shot list, understanding of when to DIY and when to hire |

**To fill your first one:**
1. Announce it on LinkedIn: "I'm running my first photography workshop in Brighton. 8 places. [Date]. [Link to book]." One post. Direct.
2. Email your existing contacts with the warm outreach template
3. Add it to your website services page and Eventbrite (free to list)

**The best thing that happens at every workshop:** attendees who understand what you do almost always book a professional session afterwards. The workshop is both revenue and a pipeline.

### TURN AMAZON AFFILIATE INTO PASSIVE INCOME

You have five blog posts live. Each one can carry legitimate affiliate links to photography equipment, books, and tools. This earns commission on every click that leads to a purchase — no extra work after setup.

Full guide: `AMAZON_AFFILIATE_INTEGRATION_DETAILED.md`  
Product CSV for WooCommerce: `SHOP_PRODUCTS_CSV_WOOCOMMERCE.csv`

**Setup (one afternoon):**
1. Apply for Amazon Associates UK: affiliate-program.amazon.co.uk (free)
2. Once approved (usually 24–72 hours), get your affiliate tag
3. Go back through each published blog post and add affiliate links to any products mentioned
4. Add a disclosure line at the top of each post: "This post contains affiliate links. I may earn a small commission if you purchase through these links, at no extra cost to you."

**Realistic income:** £150–£400/month once all posts are live and indexed. Low ceiling, but zero maintenance after setup.

### STOCK PHOTOGRAPHY — THE BACK CATALOGUE ASSET

Every image you've ever shot that isn't client-confidential is a potential stock asset. The plugin handles all the keywording. The strategy:

1. Identify 500–1,000 of your best non-client-specific images
2. Run Multi-Platform Preparation in Lightroom → select Stock Photography → Alamy
3. The plugin generates 25–50 keywords per image automatically
4. Upload to Alamy (best royalties for editorial UK work), Shutterstock, and Adobe Stock
5. Refresh with 50–100 new images every month from your shoots

Stock income compounds. It's slow at first (£50–£200/month year one) but by year two it's a consistent £500–£2,000/month from a back catalogue that keeps growing.
