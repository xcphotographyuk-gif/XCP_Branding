## BRAND POSITIONING NOTE — READ BEFORE SETTING PRICES

XCP Photography is a premium brand. The minimum 1-to-1 session rate is £1,495 and it scales up from there.

Workshop and retreat per-person prices are lower than the session rate because they are group events — but the **group revenue floor must match or exceed a standard session**. A half-day workshop of 8 people at £295/person = £2,360 gross. That is above the session floor. Never price a workshop so low that a full group generates less than £1,495.

**Marketing language always reflects the premium position:**
- Never describe workshops as "affordable" or "accessible"
- Use: "invest in your visual language", "limited places", "by application"
- Group sizes are kept deliberately small (max 8–12) — this is a positioning choice, not a capacity constraint
- Retreat language is aspirational: intimate, immersive, curated

When any selling asset (brochure, landing page, email, social post) is created for workshops or retreats, it must feel consistent with a photographer whose individual session rate starts at £1,495 — not like a generic training course.

## OVERVIEW

The most effective photographer education programmes combine free live content with paid in-person workshops and online webinars. The model works because it creates community, authority, and revenue simultaneously.

**XC Photography's equivalent:** A structured education and events programme operating across Brighton and London, with online delivery for UK-wide and international reach. Four formats. One coherent brand. Every event reinforces the message that Xavier Clarke is *the* documentary brand photographer to learn from and hire.

---

## THE STRATEGIC PURPOSE

Workshops do four things for XC Photography that client shoots alone cannot:

**1. Revenue diversification**  
A packed shoot calendar has a ceiling. Workshops scale beyond one-person capacity — teaching a group of 8 generates comparable revenue to a half-day brand session, without the post-production burden.

**2. Authority amplification**  
Teaching is the fastest route to perceived expertise. A photographer who teaches attracts clients who've already decided to trust them. Every workshop attendee becomes a potential future client or referrer.

**3. Community building**  
People who've learned together become advocates. A workshop alumni community is a referral engine and a repeat-booking base.

**4. Content creation**  
Every workshop generates photography, video, testimonials, and social content. The event *is* the content.

### Workshop Marketing Channels

**LinkedIn (primary):**  
Event announcement posts 3 weeks, 1 week, and 2 days before each event. Behind-the-scenes from the venue the day before. Testimonial post from previous attendees week of the event.

**Email list:**  
Every free webinar attendee is added to the email list (with consent). Workshop announcements go to this list first, 48 hours before public announcement. Creates VIP access feeling and builds loyalty.

**Instagram:**  
BTS content from workshops generates high engagement. Repost attendee content (with permission). Location tags for both cities.

**Google Business:**  
Post every event as a Google Business event. This improves local search visibility and reaches people searching "photography workshop Brighton" or "photography workshop London."

**Eventbrite / Luma:**  
List in-person events for discoverability beyond existing audience. Eventbrite SEO is strong for "workshop" queries.

**Brighton & London creative communities:**  
- Brighton Digital Festival (annual) — submit workshops as fringe events
- Brighton Summit — sponsor or run a session
- Creative Mornings London — speaker pitch
- Design Week London — fringe event or talk
- Photography clubs and networks — partner for promotion

### Booking System

**Platform:** Overture (if capable of event ticketing) OR Eventbrite for public events, direct invoice for corporate/bespoke.

**Confirmation flow:**
1. Booking confirmed → automated confirmation email with venue details and prep instructions
2. 1 week before → reminder email with "how to prepare" guide
3. Day before → final reminder with what to bring
4. Day after → follow-up email with resources + workshop recording link (if online) + upsell to next format (e.g. half-day attendee → offered full-day)
5. 2 weeks after → testimonial request + referral ask ("know someone who'd benefit?")

- Global scope (Brighton + London is the sweet spot; global is aspirational not operational)
