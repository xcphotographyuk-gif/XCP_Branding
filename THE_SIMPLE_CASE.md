# The Simple Case
## Why This Business Works — and What It Takes to Make It Work

---

## Start Here: What Is This Business, Actually?

Xavier is a professional photographer.

Businesses — hotels, event venues, music promoters, brands, consultants, doctors, agencies — need good photographs to show who they are and what they do. They pay a professional to take those photographs.

That's the whole model.

There is no complicated product. There is no manufacturing. There is no stock to buy. There is no app to build.

**Someone needs photographs. Xavier takes them. They pay him. Repeat.**

---

## The Evidence That Clients Will Pay

Before looking at numbers, it helps to know this isn't theoretical.

Xavier has already worked with:

- **Hotel du Vin Brighton** — one of the city's most established luxury hotel brands
- **Concorde 2, Brighton** — one of the UK's most respected live music venues (pit access for headline acts including Skunk Anansie)
- **Slack City** — Brighton-based community organisation, ongoing documentary work
- **Sussex Business Show** — regional business event coverage

These are real clients in the kinds of spaces that expect — and pay for — professional quality.

The work is proven. The question is simply how consistently he gets in front of new clients.

---

## The Money: How Does He Actually Get Paid?

There are three session types, at three price points:

| Session | What It Is | What the Client Gets | Investment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Brand Foundation** | 2 hours | 20+ edited images | **£800** |
| **Brand Elevation** | Half day | 50+ edited images, multiple setups | **£1,800** |
| **Brand Transformation** | Full day | 100+ edited images, comprehensive coverage | **£3,500** |

These aren't made-up figures. They reflect standard rates for professional brand photography in the UK.

---

## The Maths: What Does This Actually Earn?

Here is the simplest possible version:

### To earn £2,000/month:
- **3 × Brand Foundation sessions** — that's 3 clients, each paying £800
- **1 × Brand Elevation session** — that's 1 client paying £1,800

**Either of those is enough on its own.**

Three 2-hour sessions a month is not a full-time workload. It is less than one session per week.

---

### To earn £4,000/month:
- **5 × Brand Foundation sessions**, or
- **2 × Brand Elevation sessions** + **1 × Brand Foundation**, or
- **1 × Brand Transformation + 1 × Brand Foundation**

Still achievable in fewer days per month than most people work in a week.

---

### To earn £6,000–£8,000/month (realistic, consistent target):
- **3–4 sessions per week** across a mix of package types
- **Average session value of £1,500** (mix of Foundation and Elevation)

At that rate, £6,000–£8,000 is a consistent monthly outcome — without working every day, without a team, without premises.

## What "Consistent" Actually Looks Like

This is the part that matters most.

The business does not fail because Xavier can't take good photographs — that is already proven. It succeeds or fails based on whether he shows up consistently to do the non-photography parts.

Here is what consistency looks like, broken into real tasks:

### Weekly (about 3–4 hours total):

**Monday**
- Post one photograph with a short caption on Instagram and LinkedIn
- Spend 20 minutes responding to any enquiries or messages
- Identify 5 local businesses or professionals who might need photography

**Wednesday**
- Follow up on any warm leads from the previous week
- Post one behind-the-scenes shot or a piece of practical advice (takes 15 minutes)

**Friday**
- Send a direct message or email to 5 people from Monday's list
- Review the week: how many enquiries came in? How many calls booked?

**That is the minimum. It takes under 4 hours a week. It is not complicated.**

---

### Monthly (about 4–6 hours total, separate from sessions):

- Attend one local business networking event (Brighton has several every month)
- Follow up by email with everyone met at the event within 48 hours
- Check that the website contact form works and the booking link is live
- Post one longer piece of content (a short blog post, a client result, a process explained simply)

---

## The Funnel: How Enquiries Become Bookings

The numbers are simple.

If Xavier reaches out to or is seen by **100 people** in a month (through posts, networking, emails, referrals):
- About **10** will show genuine interest
- About **3** will book a paid session

That gives **3 sessions**, which at mixed pricing is roughly **£3,000–£5,000** in that month.

The only way to improve these numbers is:
1. Reach more people (consistency of outreach), or
2. Convert more of the interested ones (better follow-up), or
3. Both

Neither of these requires anything complicated. It requires showing up.

---

## The Honest Barrier: Where It Goes Wrong

This business does not fail because of the photography.

It fails — like most solo service businesses — when the person doing the work:

1. **Does a shoot, then disappears from view** until the next time they need money
2. **Doesn't follow up** on people who showed interest
3. **Waits to feel ready** before posting or reaching out
4. **Lets good weeks go by without doing the outreach tasks** above

These are not complicated mistakes. They are just human habits that need addressing.

The fix is not inspiration. The fix is a simple weekly routine that happens regardless of how motivated Xavier feels that day.

**Low motivation + routine = work gets done.**

**High motivation + no routine = bursts of effort that don't compound.**

## What Xavier Does Not Need to Make This Work

It is worth being clear about this, because people often focus on the wrong things:

❌ A perfect website before reaching out to clients  
❌ Thousands of social media followers  
❌ Expensive equipment upgrades  
❌ A studio or premises  
❌ Complex software or systems  
❌ A business partner  
❌ Large upfront investment of any kind  

**None of these are required.**

---

## What Xavier Does Need

✅ A working contact form and clear pricing on his website (already exists)  
✅ A portfolio of existing work to show (already exists)  
✅ A simple weekly routine of outreach and posting (3–4 hours/week)  
✅ A willingness to follow up with people who show interest  
✅ Consistent delivery — show up, do good work, deliver on time  

---

## The Single Deciding Factor

Everything in this document works **if Xavier shows up consistently**.

Not perfectly. Not every day. Not with full energy every week.

Just consistently. Small actions. Week after week.

The compounding effect of 3–4 hours a week of outreach, done every week for 6 months, is significant. Most people who try this stop after 3–4 weeks because they don't see immediate results. The people who keep going for 6 months find that the pipeline fills up and the income becomes predictable.

**The business is viable. The question is only whether the routine holds.**
