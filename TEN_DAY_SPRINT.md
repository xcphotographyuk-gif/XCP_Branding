### STEP 4 — Update your LinkedIn profile
**Time: 30 minutes. Effort: copy and paste.**

Your updated LinkedIn bio, headline, and experience sections are written and ready in `LINKEDIN_PROFILE_UPDATED.md`. It is word-for-word ready to paste. This matters because LinkedIn is where business clients look before they book.

---

### STEP 5 — Find your first networking event this week
**Time: 20 minutes to find it. 2-3 hours to attend.**

You are good with people in person. This is the fastest route to your first paid booking. Find one event:

- Brighton & Hove Business Club
- Any local chamber of commerce event
- Any industry mixer, launch event, or open studio
- A co-working space social evening

Go to it. Take ten business cards or have your website on your phone. Talk to people. Tell them what you do in one sentence:

> *"I'm a brand photographer based in Brighton — I work with businesses who want imagery that actually looks like them, not like a stock photo. Here's my card."*

That's it. No pitch. No pressure. Just that.

**Why now:** This is the fastest path to cash. One conversation at one event can become a booking within 48 hours. Weeks of social media posting cannot match that.

---

### STEP 6 — Email everyone you met within 24 hours
**Time: 30 minutes.**

Template is in `WARM_OUTREACH_TEMPLATES.md` under "After Networking Event". Do not wait. Memory fades. People are polite in person and distracted online. 24 hours is the window.

The email is short. It thanks them for the conversation, mentions what you do, and offers a free discovery call. Nothing more.

**Why now:** The networking only converts if you follow up. This step does not exist without Step 5.

---

### STEP 7 — Post on social media three times this week
**Time: 45 minutes total across the week.**

You are not trying to go viral. You are trying to be consistently present so that people who already know you see your name, see your work, and think of you when they need a photographer.

Three posts, Monday / Wednesday / Friday:
- Monday: one of your published blog posts (share the link + one sentence about it)
- Wednesday: one image from your portfolio with a single caption line
- Friday: "I have two sessions available in [month] — DM me if you want to talk."

Templates for all of these are in `FOR_PEOPLE_NERVOUS_ABOUT_DIGITAL.md`.

**Why now:** Social media does not generate bookings on its own. But it builds the presence that makes your follow-up emails land better. It is the background noise that makes you feel real.

---

### STEP 8 — Ask one existing client for a testimonial
**Time: 10 minutes to send the message.**

You have worked with Hotel du Vin, Concorde 2, Slack City, and Sussex Business Show. One message to one of those contacts asking for a short testimonial — what was it like to work with you, what did the images do for them — gives you real social proof that you can add to your website and use in proposals.

Template in `WARM_OUTREACH_TEMPLATES.md`.

**Why now:** A testimonial from Hotel du Vin is worth more than any amount of social media posting. It signals to every future client that someone who knows quality chose you.

---

### STEP 9 — Respond to every enquiry within 4 hours
**Time: ongoing habit.**

When someone fills in your contact form or messages you on social media, they have made a decision to reach out. That decision has a short window. If you respond the same day, you are almost certainly the only photographer they are talking to. If you respond three days later, you are one of several — and probably not the sharpest in their memory.

Set your phone to notify you when a form submission arrives. Block 30 minutes each morning to check and reply to anything that came in overnight.

**Why ongoing:** This is the single habit that turns enquiries into bookings. You can have the best website in Brighton and lose every enquiry by being slow to respond.

---

### STEP 10 — Review and repeat after 30 days
**Time: 1 hour.**

After 30 days of Steps 5–9 repeating, look at:
- How many events did you attend?
- How many follow-up emails did you send?
- How many enquiries came in?
- How many converted to bookings?

Whatever you did that produced an enquiry or a conversation — do more of it. Whatever produced nothing — change it. This is not a strategy review. It is just reading the numbers and adjusting one thing.

## PART 2 — THE BASIC VERSION

**Read this if Part 1 feels like too much. This is the minimum that actually works.**

You have a live website. You have real credentials. You need one thing: to put yourself in front of people who could hire you, and then follow up.

Here is the entire system, stripped to four actions:

---

### BASIC ACTION 1 — Attend one networking event per week
Go to it. Talk to people. Tell them what you do in one sentence (see Step 5 above). Get their business card or number.

**That's it for Action 1.**

---

### BASIC ACTION 2 — Email everyone you met the next morning
Use the template in `WARM_OUTREACH_TEMPLATES.md`. Send it before 10am the day after the event. It takes 20 minutes.

**That's it for Action 2.**

---

### BASIC ACTION 3 — Post one thing on social media twice a week
One of your blog posts. One of your images. A sentence. Anything real. It keeps you visible to people who already know you.

**That's it for Action 3.**

---

### BASIC ACTION 4 — Reply to every enquiry the same day
Phone notification on for form submissions. Reply within 4 hours where possible.

**That's it for Action 4.**

---

**The maths on the basic version:**

- 1 networking event per week = roughly 10 conversations
- 10 follow-up emails = roughly 2–3 replies
- 2–3 replies = roughly 1 discovery call
- 1 discovery call per week = roughly 1 booking per month at the start, accelerating as you get better at it
- 1 booking at Brand Foundation level = **£800**

One booking per month is not the ceiling. It is the floor. Most photographers running this consistently hit 2–4 bookings per month within 60–90 days.

**The basic version generates revenue. It is slower than doing everything in Part 1. But it works, and it is sustainable.**
