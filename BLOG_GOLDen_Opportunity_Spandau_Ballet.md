## Post copy

GOLDen opportunity.

If you look, they are everywhere. But they only show up if you do.

Tomorrow there will be a Spandau Ballet, above a pub in Brighton — painting portraits on stage, telling stories. It's a reminder that you just don't know who will show up.

Over the years I've been fortunate to photograph a wide variety of people, places and things. A Team GB gold medal-winning squash player — and their medal. Artisan makers. Insurance brokers. Palaces. Superstar DJs. Writers. Dr Who special effects make-up. Even a Jackass.

Those shoots, and the people I've met, have shaped who I am and how I shoot. They taught me to turn up, pay attention — especially with Bam — and shoot with intention.

Last year, legendary singer-songwriter Hugh Cornwell of The Stranglers played Brighton's iconic Concorde 2. A quiet reminder that golden threads of opportunity look different for everyone. Whether meeting the right person at the right time, learning a more efficient way to beat a spreadsheet, catching a promotion, discovering an event in a building you've walked past a hundred times, or taking a moment to catch a sunset you didn't plan for — they're everywhere. But only if you're present enough to notice them.

Spandau Ballet's Steve Norman will be above the actors, in Brighton. Details in the comments, or DM me.

With the evenings getting lighter, is it time to spring into action and do something different?

If you're there after the show, drop by and say hello. Whether you want to talk about the event, an upcoming portrait session, wall art, or a creative commission this season — come and find me.

Can't make it this time? You can always book a free consultation through the featured section on my profile. Let's talk about creating something that shows your audience who you actually are.

Support small venues. Grab podcast tickets in the comments.

Don't miss golden opportunities by being a great pretender. Show up. Be the authentic you.
