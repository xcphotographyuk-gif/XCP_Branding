## PART 4: BEHANCE STRATEGY

### A. Why Behance Matters

**Behance is for:**
- Portfolio showcase
- Creative community connection
- SEO (ranks in Google)
- Professional credibility
- Attracting creative industry clients

**NOT for:**
- Frequent posting (quarterly is fine)
- Direct client acquisition (mostly portfolio)
- Social engagement (low activity platform)

### B. Behance Best Practices

**1. Create a Professional Profile**

**Profile Photo:** Professional headshot
**Cover Image:** Best portfolio work
**Bio:**
```
Brand & Documentary Photographer specializing in authentic visual storytelling for businesses and creative professionals.

Based in [Your City/Region]

Website: www.xcphotography.co
Instagram: @xcphotography
Email: your@email.com
```

**Fields:**
- Brand Strategy
- Photography
- Documentary
- Portrait Photography
- Commercial Photography

**2. Create Projects (Not Individual Photos)**

**Each "Project" = One Client or Theme**

**Project Structure:**

**Title Examples:**
- "[Client Name] - Brand Photography Session"
- "Authentic Brand Photography for [Industry]"
- "Documentary-Style Business Photography - [Client]"

**Description Template:**
```
Project: Brand photography for [Client Name], a [Industry] business in [City]

Challenge:
[What the client needed - e.g., "Update outdated marketing imagery"]

Approach:
[Your process - e.g., "Documentary-style session capturing authentic team interactions"]

Solution:
[What you delivered - e.g., "50+ images showcasing their culture, space, and people"]

Result:
[Client outcome - e.g., "New imagery across website, social media, and proposals. 40% increase in consultation bookings."]

Camera: [Your gear]
Location: [City/Venue]
Client: [Industry]
Year: 2024
```

**Images:**
- 10-20 images per project
- **Best work only** (curated, not everything)
- High resolution (at least 1920px wide)
- Consistent editing style
- Tell a story with the sequence

**Tags:**
- brand photography
- [your city] photographer
- documentary photography
- business photography
- commercial photography
- [industry] photography
- authentic imagery
- professional photography

**3. Project Organization**

**Create These Project Collections:**

**Collection 1: "Brand Photography"**
- 3-5 projects of your best brand work
- Show range (different industries)
- Consistent quality

**Collection 2: "Documentary Projects"**
- Storytelling-focused work
- Event coverage
- Day-in-the-life style shoots

**Collection 3: "Portraits & Headshots"**
- Professional headshots
- Team photography
- Individual portraits

**4. Posting Frequency**

**Quarterly is plenty for Behance:**
- Q1: Add 1-2 new projects
- Q2: Update existing projects
- Q3: Add 1-2 new projects
- Q4: Refresh best projects

**Focus on quality over quantity.**

**5. Engagement Strategy**

**Weekly (15 Minutes):**
- Appreciate 10-15 projects from photographers you admire
- Follow 5 new creatives in your niche
- Respond to any comments on your work

**Monthly:**
- Update profile if needed
- Add new project
- Review analytics (what's popular?)

**6. Behance SEO Optimization**

**Project Titles Should Include:**
- Service type (Brand Photography)
- Location ([Your City])
- Industry (if applicable)

**Example:**
"Brand Photography for Tech Startups - London Commercial Photography"

**Descriptions:**
- Use keywords naturally
- Mention: brand photography, professional imagery, [city], [industry]
- Tell the story (search engines love text)

**7. Link to Your Site**

**Every project should include:**
- Website link in description
- "See more: www.xcphotography.co"
- Link to specific services page if relevant

**8. Showcase Process**

**Behance loves process:**
- Include behind-the-scenes shots
- Show lighting setup
- Share mood boards
- Include before/after (if applicable)
- Explain your approach

**Example Project Flow:**
1. Mood board/inspiration
2. Behind-the-scenes setup shot
3. 10-15 final images
4. Client testimonial (as text image)
5. Results/impact

**9. Client Testimonials**

**Create text images for testimonials:**
- Use Canva
- Quote from client
- Client name + company
- Add to end of project

**10. Behance Analytics**

**Check monthly:**
- Which projects get most views?
- Where is traffic coming from?
- What's getting appreciated?

**Double down on what works.**

## TIME-SAVING STRATEGIES

### 1. Batch Create Content

**Spend 2 hours once/month:**
- Write 12 post captions (3/week for a month)
- Create 4 carousels using templates
- Schedule everything in advance

### 2. Repurpose Content

**One blog post becomes:**
- LinkedIn article
- 3-5 Instagram carousels
- 5-10 Instagram posts
- Facebook post
- Email newsletter

**One client shoot becomes:**
- 1 Behance project
- 10+ Instagram posts
- 5+ LinkedIn posts
- Facebook album
- Blog post/case study

### 3. Use Scheduling Tools

**Free Options:**
- Meta Business Suite (Facebook + Instagram)
- LinkedIn built-in scheduling
- Later (free plan for Instagram)

**Paid Options (If scaling):**
- Buffer ($5/month)

**Use templates for:**
- Captions (see examples above)
- Carousels (`INSTAGRAM_CAROUSEL_TEMPLATES.md`)

## ANALYTICS & OPTIMIZATION

### Track Monthly:

**LinkedIn:**
- Post impressions
- Engagement rate
- Profile views
- Connection requests accepted
- **Goal:** 500+ impressions/post, 5%+ engagement

**Instagram:**
- Reach
- Engagement rate
- Profile visits
- Website clicks
- Saves (high value!)
- **Goal:** 3-5% engagement rate

**Facebook:**
- Page reach
- Post engagement
- Click-through to website
- **Goal:** Consistent group presence

**Behance:**
- Project views
- Appreciations
- Traffic to website
- **Goal:** 1,000+ views/project

### Optimize Based on Data:

**High engagement?** Create more of that type
**Low engagement?** Try different format/topic
**High saves (Instagram)?** Create more educational content
**High website clicks?** That CTA is working - use it more
